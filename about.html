Important Facts About Everest

Where is Mount Everest?

Mount Everest is located in the Mahalangur Mountain range, a section of the Himalayas in Southern Asia. It is shared by Nepal and Tibet/China, with the summit being the precise point of the International Border between the two countries.

How High is Mount Everest?

Mount Everest has the highest summit in the world, currently standing at 29,028ft (8,848m) above sea level. It is considered to be getting taller as plate tectonics are adding to its height.

Death Zone on Everest?

The "Death Zone" refers to altitudes above 26,000ft (8,000m), where the amount of oxygen is insufficient to sustain human life. Climbers face severe risks in this area.

How Many People Have Died?

As of February 16, 282 people have lost their lives on Everest. Due to harsh conditions, most bodies remain in place, making Everest a "graveyard" for climbers.

Difficulty Breathing?

Due to low oxygen levels, climbers experience severe difficulty breathing, requiring supplemental oxygen. Without acclimatization, one could collapse within minutes.

How Cold is it at the Peak?

In January, the coldest month, temperatures drop to -60°C, while in July, they average -19°C. The peak temperature never rises above 0°C.

Isn’t Everest the Tallest?

While Mount Everest has the highest altitude, Mauna Kea in Hawaii is taller when measured from base to peak, with a height of 33,500ft.

Who is "Green Boots"?

"Green Boots" is an iconic body landmark believed to belong to Indian climber Tsewang Paljor. It has remained in place since 1996.
